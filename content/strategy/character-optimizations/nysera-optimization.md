# Nysera - Optimization Guide

## Current Build Summary

**High Elf Circle of the Land (Forest) Druid 3** | WIS 15 (+2) | DEX 16 (+3) | CON 15 (+2)

Currently prepared: Control Flames, Speak with Animals, Poison Spray, Charm Person, Enhance Ability, Flaming Sphere, Moonbeam, Fire Bolt

Druids prepare spells from their full class list after every long rest. This is the biggest optimization lever available - the current spell list is not optimized for a cave environment with enemy groups.

## Priority Changes

### 1. Prepare Spells After Every Long Rest

This is not optional. Druids can change their entire prepared list each morning. A spell that was useful last week may be wrong for the current dungeon. Before the next session starts, look at what's ahead - tight cave corridors, groups of enemies, possible ambushes - and prepare accordingly.

Set a habit: long rest ends, spells get updated.

### 2. Swap Speak with Animals for Spike Growth

Spike Growth (2nd-level, concentration): 20-foot radius of difficult terrain that deals 2d4 piercing damage per 5 feet moved through it. In a cave corridor, enemies cannot avoid walking through it to reach the party. An ogre crossing 30 feet of Spike Growth takes 12d4 damage just moving.

This is the single strongest spell for cave chokepoints on the druid list.

### 3. Swap Charm Person for Healing Word

Healing Word is on the druid list. Bonus action, 1d4+WIS healing at 60-foot range. With Elaris and Selena both having Healing Word, a third caster with access gives the party redundancy. If both of them go down, Nysera can still pull someone back with a bonus action. Charm Person requires humanoids and is contested by WIS - it won't work on most cave enemies.

### 4. Consider Adding Command to Prepared List

Command (1st-level): one word, WIS save or the target must obey. "Flee", "Grovel", "Drop", "Halt". Against ogres with modest WIS, DC 12 (WIS +2 + proficiency) is beatable. "Grovel" makes the ogre go prone - advantage for all melee attackers. Costs a 1st-level slot, action economy is clean.

## Suggested Prepared List for Cave Environment

| Spell | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **Spike Growth** | Area denial in corridors. High damage on movement. |
| **Moonbeam** | Keep this - 2d10 radiant per turn, damages creatures that enter or start their turn in it. Consistent damage on a controlled area. |
| **Healing Word** | Backup healer with bonus action recovery. |
| **Flaming Sphere** | Keep this - bonus action to move, 2d6 fire on contact, area threat. Good sustained damage. |
| **Command** | Cheap action economy. "Grovel" or "Flee" on high-priority targets. |
| **Enhance Ability** | Situational but keeps. Advantage on checks is useful in skill encounters. |
| **Thunderwave** | Strong push-back spell for cave entry points. 15-foot cube, CON save, pushes enemies 10 feet and causes 2d8 thunder. |

## Circle of the Land (Forest) Features

Forest druids get bonus spells including Barkskin and Spider Climb at level 3. Spider Climb is excellent in a cave - ignore difficult terrain, climb on walls and ceilings. Consider preparing it if exploration is expected.

Natural Recovery: recover expended spell slots during a short rest (up to a combined total equal to half druid level, rounded up = 1 spell slot level at level 3). Use this to recover Healing Word or Command slots mid-dungeon.

## Next Level-Up Plan

Level 4 opens **Wild Shape improvement** and another ASI/feat slot. +2 WIS takes you to 17 (+3) for better spell DCs and saves. Alternatively, **War Caster** keeps concentration on Spike Growth or Moonbeam when taking hits - druids have low AC and both high-value spells require concentration.
