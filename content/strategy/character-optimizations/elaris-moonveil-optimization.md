# Elaris Moonveil - Optimization Guide

## Current Build Summary

**Half-Elf College of Lore Bard 3** | CHA 20 (+5) | Spell DC 15 | DEX 18 (+4)

Current spells: Starry Wisp, Mending, Charm Person, Hideous Laughter, Speak with Animals, Identify, Healing Word, Bless

Elaris is the party's best controller. CHA 20 at level 3 is excellent, and DC 15 is hard for low-WIS creatures like ogres to beat. The main issue is two spells are doing near-zero work in this environment.

## Priority Changes

### 1. Swap Identify for Silvery Barbs (reaction, 1st-level)

**This is the most impactful change available to the party right now.**

Silvery Barbs: as a reaction when a creature succeeds on an attack roll, saving throw, or ability check, force them to reroll and take the lower result. An ally of your choice then has advantage on their next roll.

The ogre that downed Theren landed a critical hit. Silvery Barbs can be used in response to a crit (a crit is a successful attack roll - a 20 is still a "success"). Force the reroll, the crit disappears.

Identify is not lost - Elaris can ritual cast it in 10 minutes using no spell slot. Barzer can also ritual cast Identify. Dropping it from the prepared list costs nothing in practice.

### 2. Swap Speak with Animals for Faerie Fire (1st-level, concentration)

Faerie Fire: 20-foot cube, all creatures in range must succeed on a DEX save or become outlined in light. While outlined, attack rolls against them have **advantage** - from everyone, all at once, for the whole fight.

Ogres have bad DEX saves. In a dark cave this also lights them up visually. A single casting of Faerie Fire on a group of enemies gives the entire party advantage on every attack until concentration drops. That's more total damage than almost anything else on the spell list at this level.

Speak with Animals is a ritual spell (no slot needed) and has no use in a combat-heavy cave environment.

### 3. Swap Charm Person for Dissonant Whispers (1st-level)

Dissonant Whispers: 3d6 psychic damage (WIS save for half), and on a failed save the target must use its reaction to move away from you as far as it can. That forced movement **provokes opportunity attacks** from Kaltor.

Psychic damage is resisted by almost nothing. A failed WIS save means Kaltor gets a free swing as the enemy runs. Charm Person requires humanoid targets, is useless in most dungeon fights, and the "not useful mid-dungeon" problem isn't going away.

## Spells to Keep

| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Healing Word** | Bonus action, 60-foot range. Saved Theren's life. Never drop this. |
| **Hideous Laughter** | DC 15 is very hard for ogres (low WIS). Prone + incapacitated = advantage for melee attackers and auto-fail STR/DEX saves. |
| **Bless** | +1d4 to attacks and saves for 3 allies, concentration. Best opening move when the whole party is engaged. Stacks with everything. |
| **Starry Wisp** | Solid cantrip - ranged attack, no components issue in caves. |

## Feature Usage Tips

**Cutting Words** - This is free and aggressive. As a reaction, roll a Bardic Inspiration die (d6 at level 3) and subtract the result from an enemy's attack roll, ability check, or damage roll. You see the roll before deciding.

Use it to turn a hit into a miss. Use it against damage rolls on a downed ally. The dice are free with a short rest - spend them.

**Bardic Inspiration** - Give dice out at the start of combat, not when someone is already down. D6 added to an attack or save at the right moment is more valuable than a healing word in most cases.

## Next Level-Up Plan

Level 4 gives an Ability Score Improvement or feat. CHA is already at 20 - consider **War Caster** (advantage on concentration saves, cast spells as opportunity attacks) or **Resilient: CON** (proficiency on CON saves, which are concentration saves). Faerie Fire and Bless are both concentration - keeping them up in melee range matters.
