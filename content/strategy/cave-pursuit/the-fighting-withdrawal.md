Retreat from deeper in the cave is harder than retreat from the entrance. You now have to cross the open cavern area AND the bottleneck before you're outside. The path back is longer, and the missing ogre could be between you and the exit right now.

This plan exists so everyone knows their role before the retreat starts - not during it.

## Retreat Trigger Conditions

Call the retreat **immediately** if any of these occur:

- [ ] Two or more party members drop to 0 HP in the same encounter
- [ ] An unknown threat appears that cannot be identified
- [ ] Kaltor drops below 8 HP with no Selena heals remaining
- [ ] A fiend appears that isn't an imp or minor creature
- [ ] The party is flanked - enemies approaching from both deeper in AND from behind
- [ ] Spell slots are critically low across multiple casters
- [ ] **The missing ogre appears behind the party** - this is a cut-off scenario and needs immediate response

**Who Calls It**: Anyone. If Theren calls it, it happens - no debate. The player most aware of the party's position calls it.

**The Signal**: "**Fall back!**" - short, unambiguous. Agree on this before moving.

## The Cut-Off Scenario

The missing ogre could be behind you. It may have circled around during the advance. If the party is attacked from the rear while already engaged at the front, this is the most dangerous situation:

- Do NOT split to fight both groups simultaneously
- **Barzer uses Fog Cloud** immediately on the rear threat - blind them, buy time
- The party consolidates on Kaltor's position and decides: break through the rear threat toward the exit, or push through the front threat toward the unknown
- **Default**: Break toward the exit. The cave interior is unknown; the exit is known

## Retreat Path

You are deeper than before. The retreat is in two stages.

**Stage 1: Cross the Open Cavern**
- This is the exposed part. Enemies in an open space can chase you from any angle
- Move fast, stay together, do not let anyone fall behind

**Stage 2: Through the Bottleneck**
- The dead ogre is at the bottleneck - it's not blocking you, but the body is there
- The bottleneck now works in your favor on retreat: pursuing enemies are funneled the same way they funneled you
- Kaltor holds the bottleneck from the other side while the rest of the party exits

**Stage 3: Outside**
- Rally point is outside the cave entrance, 30ft minimum from the mouth
- Enough distance that a pursuing enemy can't instantly exit and reach you

## Retreat Order

```
              [DEEPER CAVE - threat direction]
                            |
                     [BARZER] ← Fog Cloud, then run immediately
                            |
              [NYSERA] ← Wild Shape or Disengage
                            |
              [SELENA] ← move fast, she exits early
                            |
              [ELARIS] ← Healing Word while moving
                            |
              [THEREN] ← covering fire, shortbow at close range
                            |
                     [KALTOR] ← last out through the bottleneck, Disengage

              [OPEN CAVERN → BOTTLENECK → OUTSIDE]
```

**Key change from the original plan**: Barzer exits first now, not after the squishies. At his HP level (even after healing), he dies if caught. Get him out. Kaltor's AC and HP makes him the right choice for rear guard.

## Role-by-Role Retreat Instructions

### Barzer
- **Fog Cloud** centered between the party and the primary threat - this is your first action on the retreat round, before you move
- Fog Cloud radius is 20ft. Creatures inside are heavily obscured - they're effectively blind. Even creatures with darkvision cannot see through Fog Cloud
- After casting: **Misty Step** if you're in danger of being caught, then sprint for the exit
- You are fragile. Do not stop. Do not help someone else unless it's a Healing Word (Barzer doesn't have Healing Word, so: do not stop)

### Kaltor
- Use **Disengage** action to exit without provoking opportunity attacks
- If you can't Disengage safely, eat the opportunity attack and run - you have the HP for it
- **Hold the bottleneck exit** once you reach it: stand in the narrow point, let the rest of the party get past you, then Disengage out
- You are the door. You are the last one out

### Nysera
- **Wild Shape** into a CR 0 animal if spell slots are exhausted - run in animal form, harder to target, faster in tight spaces
- If you have Wild Shape HP remaining from an earlier use, shift into it now to tank one more hit
- Drop concentration spells immediately - freeing mental bandwidth matters on a chaotic retreat
- Move toward the exit; do not slow down for anything

### Elaris
- **Healing Word** as a bonus action while moving - does not require stopping, 60ft range, targets Kaltor or Theren if they're injured
- Priority: Kaltor first if he's below 12 HP (he's the bottleneck key), then Theren
- **Hideous Laughter** on a pursuing enemy if the retreat is about to collapse - one round of incapacitation on the closest pursuer can save the party
- Move fast. Your job is to be out and healing from outside, not fighting a rearguard action

### Theren
- **Covering fire** with Shortbow or Longbow as you move - +4 Dex means you hit while running
- **Disengage** if an enemy is adjacent before firing
- **Perception +5**: Watch for threats approaching from the cave entrance side during the retreat. You may spot the Fog Cloud exit point before it clears. Call it
- Your job is to get out and watch the entrance from outside - your Perception makes you the best person to know if the cave is still pursuing

### Selena
- Move. You are not the rear guard.
- **Sanctuary** on yourself if enemies target you - forces a Wisdom save before any attack
- **Sanctuary on Barzer** if he's being chased - he dies from one hit
- Save remaining spell slots for post-retreat triage outside

## The Bottleneck on Retreat

The bottleneck that was a threat on the way in is an asset on the way out.

Once the first party members are through it and outside, Kaltor turns and holds it. Pursuing enemies face the same funnel that the party had to navigate. Kaltor holds for one or two rounds - time enough for everyone to get clear - then Disengages out.

**Do not skip this step.** An enemy that pours through the bottleneck into the open outside can pursue effectively. One round of Kaltor holding the exit is worth it.

## Rally Point

**Outside the cave entrance, 30ft minimum from the cave mouth.**

Do not rally at the cave mouth - a pursuing enemy exits and is immediately in melee. Put distance between you and the cave before stopping.

Once outside:

1. Selena assesses injuries - triage in order of severity (Barzer first by default)
2. Barzer counts remaining spell slots across the party
3. Decide: short rest vs. return to town for long rest
4. No one goes back in alone. No one goes back in until the party is stable and a plan exists

## Post-Retreat Decision

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Party mostly healthy, ogres cleared | Short rest (1 hour), re-enter with a new approach |
| Heavy casualties, slots spent | Return to town for long rest before re-entry |
| Captives confirmed inside | Send for help - do not re-enter short-handed or depleted |
| Infernal source confirmed | Long rest mandatory - do not face an infernal threat on a depleted party |
| Missing ogre is between you and exit | This was a cut-off - Fog Cloud, Misty Step, and Kaltor anchor. Fight through or go deeper and find another exit |

## Pros
- Everyone knows their role before things go wrong
- Fog Cloud buys time against pursuers (works against darkvision - even creatures that can see in darkness cannot see through Fog Cloud)
- Kaltor's HP is the retreat buffer
- The bottleneck on exit funnels any pursuit and lets Kaltor hold it temporarily
- Healing Word works while moving - no action economy lost

## Cons
- Retreat path is longer now - open cavern to bottleneck to outside
- If the missing ogre is behind you, the path may not be clear
- Captives (if any) are left behind during withdrawal
- The infernal presence will know you were there and fled
- Barzer is critically fragile - any delay in his exit could be fatal

## Best If
The party agreed on the retreat signal before advancing, Barzer is positioned near the back of the formation so his exit path is short, and Kaltor is in position to hold the bottleneck from the outside for 1-2 rounds of cover.
