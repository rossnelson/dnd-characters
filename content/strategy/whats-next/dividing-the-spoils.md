## Ring of Jumping

This is the real decision. Who benefits most?

### Candidate: Kaltor (Paladin)
**For:**
- Frontline fighter who needs to close distance
- 30 ft speed - slowest in heavy armor
- Could leap into combat to protect squishier party members

**Against:**
- Already has strong magic items (Amulet of Health, +1 Longsword)
- As the tank, he wants enemies to come to him

### Candidate: Theren (Ranger)
**For:**
- Two-weapon fighting means he needs to be in melee range
- Can switch between ranged and melee flexibly
- Wood Elf already has 35 ft speed - the ring amplifies good mobility

**Against:**
- Already mobile with elf speed
- Prefers ranged combat with longbow

### Candidate: Elaris (Bard)
**For:**
- **Got surrounded this battle** - needs escape options
- High DEX (+3) means she can capitalize on mobility
- Can cast Identify to confirm the ring's properties

**Against:**
- Could use spell slots for mobility spells instead
- Not typically in melee

### Candidate: Selena (Cleric)
**For:**
- Primary healer needs to survive
- Escape option if enemies target the healer

**Against:**
- Should be positioned safely in the back line anyway

### Not Recommended
- **Barzer** - Already has flight capability
- **Nysera** - Druid has Wild Shape for mobility

---

## Recommendations

### Most Practical
**Give the ring to Kaltor.** He's the slowest party member and needs to engage enemies quickly to protect the group. Heavy armor limits his mobility, and the ring compensates for that.

### Based on Recent Events
**Give the ring to Elaris.** She was surrounded in the last battle. The ring gives her an escape route when things go wrong.

### Compromise
Let Elaris use the ring for now. If she finds she doesn't need it, pass it to Kaltor.
